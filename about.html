About Occult Oddities

A studio for curiosity, creativity, and personal symbolism.

We make thoughtful digital publications for people interested in tarot, journaling, art, symbolism, and modern forms of personal practice.

The Story

A place between the sketchbook and the altar.

Occult Oddities began with a simple observation: many beautiful spiritual resources ask people to adopt a fixed system before they are invited to explore.

We wanted to create something different—a collection of artful tools that treats symbols as starting points, tarot as a language of reflection, and journaling as a form of creative inquiry.

The result is part digital apothecary, part independent press, and part working sketchbook. Every release is made to be used, marked, rearranged, interpreted, and made personal.

Our Values

Made with room for interpretation.

Our work is grounded in openness, accessibility, and respect for the many ways people understand art and personal practice.

Secular

No spiritual claim is presented as universal fact. Our tools can be used psychologically, creatively, symbolically, spiritually, or simply for enjoyment.

Inclusive

People of all identities, backgrounds, bodies, belief systems, and experience levels are welcome here. Curiosity is enough.

Accessible

We avoid gatekeeping and unnecessary jargon. Resources are designed to be approachable without flattening their subjects.

Artist-Led

Visual language matters. Typography, illustration, layout, and physical-feeling details are part of the experience—not an afterthought.

Curiosity-Driven

We value good questions more than fixed answers and encourage readers to notice how meanings change across contexts.

Personal

A meaningful practice should become your own. Our pages leave room for annotation, contradiction, adaptation, and discovery.

Meet the Maker

Created slowly, studied closely.

Occult Oddities is an independent digital studio shaped by a love of fine-press books, artist zines, marginalia, tarot imagery, botanical illustration, and the strange visual histories of everyday objects.

Each release begins with research and sketching, then moves through writing, illustration, layout, testing, and revision. The goal is to make something beautiful enough to keep and useful enough to return to.
